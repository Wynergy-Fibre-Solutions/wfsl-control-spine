WFSL LICENSING FRAMEWORK

Purpose
- Define licence posture per WFSL asset
- Separate open, source-available, and commercial rights
- Provide clarity for investors, partners, and customers

Licence Classes
1. Open Source
   - Permissive where appropriate
   - Used for tooling, libraries, and community adoption
   - No commercial warranty implied

2. Source-Available
   - Code visible for audit and trust
   - Restricted commercial use
   - Licence terms defined by WFSL

3. Commercial
   - Licensed for production use
   - Includes support, updates, and assurances
   - Subject to contract

Asset Mapping
- WyneOS
  Licence: Commercial (with audit visibility)
  Notes: Investor-reviewed, frozen snapshot available

- Verification & Integrity Tooling
  Licence: Source-Available transitioning to Commercial
  Notes: Audit-first posture

- Testing Frameworks
  Licence: Open Source core, Commercial extensions
  Notes: Community adoption with enterprise uplift

Governance
- Licence changes require Control Spine update
- All licences must be explicit and versioned
